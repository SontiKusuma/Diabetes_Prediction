Understanding HbA1c

Hemoglobin A1c Test Information

What is HbA1c?

HbA1c, or Hemoglobin A1c, is a blood test that measures your average blood sugar levels over the past 2-3 months. It's also known as the A1c test or glycated hemoglobin test.

Why is it important?

  • Measures long-term glucose control
  • Helps diagnose prediabetes and diabetes
  • Monitors diabetes treatment effectiveness
  • Predicts risk of diabetes complications

HbA1c Levels Interpretation

HbA1c Level Category Description
Below 5.7% Normal No diabetes
5.7% to 6.4% Prediabetes Increased risk of diabetes
6.5% or above Diabetes Diagnosis confirmed

Target Ranges for Diabetes Management

  • General Target: Below 7%
  • Tighter Control: 6.5% or lower (if possible without hypoglycemia)
  • Less Stringent: Below 8% (for older adults or those with complications)

Tips for Better HbA1c

  • Regular blood glucose monitoring
  • Balanced diet with controlled carbohydrates
  • Regular physical activity
  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Regular medical check-ups
